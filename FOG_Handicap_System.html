How Your Handicap is Calculated

The FOG Golf League uses the official GHIN/World Handicap System (WHS) formula with real USGA Course Ratings and Slope Ratings for every course we play. Your handicap is automatically calculated from your scoring history and updates every time new scores are entered.

Step 1: Score Differentials

For each round you've played, we calculate a score differential using the official USGA formula:

Score Differential = (Score − Course Rating) × (113 ÷ Slope Rating)

Course Rating = how a scratch golfer is expected to score (accounts for difficulty beyond just par)

Slope Rating = how much harder the course is for a bogey golfer vs. a scratch golfer (113 = average)

113 = the standard "neutral" slope used to normalize across courses

Example

You shoot 44 on the front 9 at Murphy Creek (9-hole Rating: 34.7, Slope: 126):

(44 − 34.7) × (113 ÷ 126) = 9.3 × 0.897 = 8.3

Your differential for that round is 8.3

This formula means a score of 44 at a harder course (high slope) gives you a lower differential than the same score at an easy course. It levels the playing field across different courses.

Step 2: Minimum Rounds Required

You need at least 5 rounds on file to establish a handicap. We use your last 2 seasons of scoring history (2024, 2025, and the current season). Until you have 5 rounds, your handicap shows as 0.

We use your best (lowest) differentials. How many depends on your total rounds:

Step 4: Calculate Your Handicap Index

Handicap Index = Average of Best Differentials + Adjustment

Truncated (not rounded) to one decimal place. Range: 0.0 to 54.0

Full Example

Player with 10 Rounds Across 2 Seasons

After applying the GHIN formula to each round, suppose your 10 differentials are:

12.1,  8.4,  5.9,  14.2,  9.0,  7.3,  11.5,  4.8,  10.6,  13.7

  1. Sort best to worst: 4.8, 5.9, 7.3, 8.4, 9.0, 10.6, 11.5, 12.1, 13.7, 14.2
  2. With 10 rounds, use best 3: 4.8, 5.9, 7.3
  3. Average: (4.8 + 5.9 + 7.3) ÷ 3 = 6.0
  4. No adjustment for 10 rounds
  5. Handicap Index = 6.0

How Net Scores Work

Net Score = Gross Score − Handicap

Example: You shoot 48 with a handicap of 10 → Net = 38

Net scores level the playing field — a higher-handicap player can compete fairly against a lower-handicap player.

Key Rules

  • Automatic updates: Your handicap recalculates every time new scores are entered. No action needed.
  • 2-year scoring window: Scores from 2024, 2025, and the current season are all included.
  • Minimum 5 rounds: You need 5 rounds before a handicap kicks in.
  • Best rounds matter most: The system uses your best performances, not your worst days.
  • Course difficulty matters: The same score at a harder course gives you a better (lower) differential.
  • Outlier protection: Any single-round differential is capped at 40 to prevent one bad round from wrecking your handicap.
  • Admin override: League admins can manually adjust a handicap if needed (shown with *).

Where to See Your Handicap

  • Season Standings: HCP column shows everyone's handicap. Tap your name for a full breakdown.
  • Player Roster: Shows HCP next to your name.
  • Weekly Results: Shows Gross, vs Par, HCP, and Net for each week.
How is this different from the official GHIN app? Our system uses the same core formula — (Score − Course Rating) × 113/Slope — with real USGA course ratings for every course we play. The main simplification is that we use the 18-hole course rating divided by 2 for our 9-hole rounds (the official GHIN app has separate 9-hole ratings from the USGA database). The result is very close to what the GHIN app would calculate.
